Grouping data can help you deal with continuous values. For instance, I make an Emergency Level column to indicate the emergency level according to daily New Cases. DATA VISUALIZATION

COVID-19 Dashboard Overview

As a Data Analyst, the ability to communicatee our results clearly and accurately to other people, especially to our company's shareholders, has become an essential role for the job. Even though it sounds easy, the intricacy of choosing the correct graph and displaying the information is key to avoid any misinformation. What I like to do, is to keep the design as simple as possible and avoid adding unnecessary information to keep the audience focused only on the most important part. The main idea of this project is to mimic the dashboard created by the WHO website on COVID-19 (link: https://data.who.int/dashboards/covid19/cases?n=c).

COVID-19 Geographical Chart

The geographical chart represents the total number of New Cases recorded around Indonesia. Each area has its unique color that represents its emergency level based on the daily New Cases on the area. The green zone represents a safe zone where the daily cases are lower, while the red zone represents the danger zone where the daily cases are high. If you hover your mouse over a specific area on the map, the detailed information will pop out and show the exact value of the data.

COVID-19 Time-Series Chart

The time-series chart records the development of the COVID-19 virus throughout the time. On the far left, the graph represents the total cases that occurred on 03 January 2020. On the far right, the graph represents the total cases that occurred on 16 September 2022. Both values on the upper left and lower left show the specific values for the Total New Cases and Total New Deaths, respectively. The time series chart here could also be used as a filter to find out the transmission of COVID-19 around Indonesia.

COVID-19 Horizontal Bar Chart

The last chart, the horizontal bar chart, represents the top 3 areas that have the highest cases of COVID-19. This chart can help the government to understand which area is in critical condition and needs immediate help. It helps us to learn about our priorities and take action to react to those circumstances.
